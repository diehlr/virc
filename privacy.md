# Privacy Policy for v.irc

**Last updated: April 18, 2026**

## Summary

v.irc is a macOS IRC client that stores all data locally on your device. We do not operate any servers that collect, transmit, or store your personal information. The only network traffic generated by the app is the direct connection between your computer and the IRC servers you choose to connect to.

## Who operates v.irc

v.irc is an independent app developed and distributed by a solo developer (referred to as "the developer," "we," "us," or "our" in this policy). This policy explains how the app handles your information.

## What information v.irc handles

**Information you provide to the app:**

- A username or handle that you configure for use on IRC networks
- IRC server addresses, channel names, and other connection settings you enter
- Messages you send and receive through IRC
- Any notes, preferences, or customizations you save within the app

**Information the app automatically generates:**

- Local application preferences and cache files necessary for the app to function
- Connection logs if you enable logging features

## Where your information is stored

All information handled by v.irc is stored locally on your Mac. This includes your username, server settings, message history, and any other data the app needs. **None of this information is transmitted to the developer or to any third-party servers operated by the developer.**

Your data is stored in the standard macOS application support directory (typically `~/Library/Application Support/v.irc/` or similar). You can delete this data at any time by removing these files or uninstalling the app.

## Third-party services

v.irc does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, crash-reporting, or tracking services.

When you use v.irc to connect to an IRC network, you are establishing a direct network connection between your Mac and the server operator you have chosen. The developer of v.irc has no relationship with those IRC server operators, does not control them, and cannot see or access the data you exchange with them. When you connect to an IRC server, that server operator may record information about your connection (such as your IP address, nickname, channels joined, and messages sent to public channels) according to that server's own policies. You should review the privacy practices of any IRC network you connect to.

## What we do not do

- We do not operate any servers that receive, store, or process your data
- We do not collect analytics, usage statistics, or telemetry
- We do not use crash-reporting SDKs that send data off your device
- We do not display advertising
- We do not sell, rent, share, or disclose your information — because we do not have it
- We do not require you to create an account with us to use the app

## Children's privacy

v.irc is not directed to children under the age of 13, and the developer does not knowingly collect any personal information from children. Because the app does not collect personal information from any users, it does not collect any from children either.

## Security

Because your data stays on your device, its security depends primarily on the security of your Mac. We recommend keeping your macOS system up to date, using a strong account password, and enabling FileVault disk encryption.

Data sent over IRC connections may or may not be encrypted depending on the server you connect to. When available, v.irc supports TLS-encrypted connections to IRC servers (typically port 6697). Plain-text IRC connections (typically port 6667) are not encrypted and should not be used for sensitive information.

## Changes to this policy

If this policy is updated, the "Last updated" date at the top of the policy will be changed. For material changes to how the app handles data, notice will be provided through the app's release notes or on the app's website. Continued use of v.irc after such changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
